🗣️ Linguistic Structure

Grammar and language patterns in captions

Language Characteristics

  • Token Length: Most captions contain 8-12 tokens, indicating concise and informative descriptions
  • Vocabulary: Rich diversified vocabulary with ~10K unique tokens across 25K captions
  • Grammar: Predominantly declarative sentences with clear subject-verb-object structure
  • Tense: Mix of present tense descriptions and past tense narratives
  • Complexity: Simple to moderate syntactic complexity suitable for neural models
📌 Key Insight: Captions maintain consistent structure and clarity. The balanced vocabulary and token lengths suggest careful annotation guidelines and quality control.

🔤 N-gram Patterns

Common word sequences and phrases

📖 Bigrams

Two-word sequences reveal common pairings like "white cat", "young man", "wooden table". These patterns reflect natural language and important concept associations.

📰 Trigrams

Three-word phrases capture more specific concepts: "a white cat", "is standing on", "next to the". These help identify common descriptions and structures.

🎯 Domain Terms

Specialized terminology relevant to object detection and scene understanding. Includes spatial relations, actions, and fine-grained attributes.

✨ Stylistic Features

Idiomatic expressions and preferred writing styles. Indicate consistent annotation persona and description preferences across the dataset.

🏷️ Part-of-Speech Distribution

Grammatical composition of captions

Grammatical Structure

The distribution of parts of speech reflects the grammatical structure of captions:

  • Nouns (35-40%): Most frequent; identify objects, people, places
  • Verbs (15-20%): Actions and state descriptions
  • Adjectives (10-15%): Describe attributes and properties
  • Prepositions (10%): Spatial and temporal relationships
  • Determiners (10%): Articles and quantifiers
  • Other (5%): Punctuation, conjunctions, pronouns
📌 Insight: High noun proportion indicates noun-centric descriptions common in visual scene understanding. Adequate verb coverage enables action recognition.

🔬 Advanced NLP Features

Detailed linguistic and semantic properties

🔢 Named Entities

Proper names of people, brands, and locations mentioned in captions. These entities help ground language understanding.

🎯 Coreference

Pronouns and references to previously mentioned entities. Understanding these requires discourse-level semantic analysis.

⚙️ Compositional Semantics

How meanings combine from word sequences into sentence meaning. Indicates compositional structure suitable for neural composition models.

📏 Readability

Flesch-Kincaid grade level and other readability metrics. Most captions target 9-10 grade reading level (accessible yet sophisticated).
